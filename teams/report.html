Report


What does the report team do?

The report team is primarily responsible for presenting UCSD DBF’s vision for the plane, including our design and fabrication processes, to the competition organizers. We write a short design proposal by the end of October to formally enter the competition, and a technical report by mid-February that is crucial to a team’s overall performance.

Because the quality of our work directly determines whether UCSD DBF makes the competition and how well we score, we’ve been rated the “most underrated sub-team” by our project managers!

As a report team member, you will:

  • Brush up on your technical writing skills, by using best-practices to convey scientific and engineering information in a clear, concise manner
  • Develop professional relationships and networking skills, by communicating with members of other sub-teams to help in the writing process
  • Use MATLAB (and similar programs) to write code, keep data, and create figures
  • Utilize graphic design principles to enhance and supplement the content.

If you don’t have experience with these skills now, don’t worry-- you will learn them! Contact dbf at ucsd dot edu to join!
